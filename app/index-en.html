Violence Evolution in Ciudad Juárez, Mexico

En colaboración con Codeando México

Between 1990-2005 the murders of more than 300 women made this border Mexican city infamous. Most of the victims were young maquila workers, nabbed from the streets to not be seen again, their bodies dumped in the city’s outskirts. But Juarez agony got worse between 2007-2010, when the city became the fighting ground for the Juarez and Sinaloa cartels who battled for control of trafficking routes. More than 11 thousand people, mostly youth, died in the conflict. The city became the world’s murder capital, with 250 homicides per month in a city of 1.5 million.

Then in January 2010, the city hit bottom. On January 31, 2010, during a youth gathering at a working class neighborhood of maquila workers, a killing commando busted in and murdered 11 youth and four adults. Juarez’s La Linea cartel believed they belonged to an enemy group, but in the end they made a mistake, the youth were students and not sicarios.

On February 11, 2010, the mother of two of the young victims challenged then President Felipe Calderon who came to visit Juarez to face the families after the massacre:

"I cannot welcome you here, because to me you are not welcome. None of you are welcome. Because for more than two years, we have seen murders here. Many things have occurred and nobody has done anything to stop it."

And that was true. Mayhem in Juarez was increasing. It reached the limit in 2010.

Violence Evolution

  • 1990

  • January

  • Feminicides

    Juárez is internationally known for a string of disappearances and brutal murders of young women which began occurring in the early 90s. More than 300 women were killed between 1990 and 2005.

  • 2006

  • January

  • 2 or 3 Killings per day

    The city was controlled by the Juárez Cartel led by Vicente Carrillo. One to two murders were reported per day.

  • November

  • Violence Arises

    The violence started to increase. Murders rose by 10 percent as compared to numbers for 2005, according to statistics of the then State Agency of Criminal Research.

  • December

  • Clandestine wells

    First bodies of disappeared victims were found buried in clandestine hidden wells. Police units become more and more infiltrated with organized crime.

  • 2007

  • February

  • Insecurity perception

    Citizen perception of insecurity hit a high 33 percent.

  • March

  • Shootings

    Shootings occur in highly transited streets and avenues and during daytime.

  • June

  • Silent manifestations

    Silent marches begin to be organized after the death of a university student. Professors, students and parents asked for better security.

  • July

  • Curfew

    Municipal police implement a curfew that begins at 10 pm. The curfew helps prevent youth from participatinng in illicit and gang activities."After 10 PM, at home is better" because younglings start getting involved in delictive activities and gangs.

  • Killing of 5 policemen

    Five police from the stolen car section in the Policia Ministerial, or state police, are found murdered and stuffed in the trunk of a car.

  • Narcomessages

    Narco-messages are left on cardboad signs next to bodies of victims. Some of the messages read: "Promises are respected" and "For those who do not not believe it."

  • August

  • El Diego takes Control

    José Antonio Acosta Hernández, alías El Diego takes control of the Cartel de Juarez armed group "La Línea". He begins recruiting young followers.

  • Method of Death

    Bodies exhibiting extreme torture and handcuffed are found wrapped in plastic bags.

  • Levantones rise up

    Kidnappings increase. In the Acequias upper-class neighborhood, 12 bodies are found. Members of the Ministerial police, or state police, are found guilty of those killings.

  • Deaths, from 3 to 7 per day

    Daily murders rise from three to seven.

  • September

  • Juárez, Route for Arms

    U.S. authorities identify Juárez as a direct arms trafficking route from the United States for 22 mm and 38 mm guns.

  • November

  • Directors of companies ask for intervention

    The business community asks for the Mexican Federal government to intervene in Juarez.

  • December

  • Crime rises

    The year closes with 9 thousand cars reported stolen and 60 criminal gangs operating in the city.

  • New system of justice

    A new system of penal justice tailored after the U.S. is introduced.

  • 2008

  • January

  • Narcowar starts

    The narco war begins to use tactics that terrorize the civilian population in the city.

  • Generalized Fear

    The city was observing the beginning of a war. Fear was spreading among city residents. This was the first month with a toll of 42 people murdered.

  • February

  • Deaths of Policemen rises

    More police officers get killed.

  • May

  • More deaths in 2007

    16 policemen are killed this month, beating records in 2007 when 14 were killed.

  • Army intervention

    500 Mexican Army troops arrive in the city to enforce the Joint Operation Chihuahua

  • Don't visit Juarez

    The U.S. State Department issues a travel advisory to U.S. citizens who want to visit Juarez.

  • Narcomessages

    Narco wall messages begin appearing all over the city. Both cartels list police officers who are to be killed soon.

  • June

  • Medical Services Collapse

    Medical services in the city collapse as even with military protection, narcos continue to attack medical installations.

  • July

  • "Quita Puercos"

    The "Eliminate Swine" Internet page appears. The page lists names of policemen working with the Juarez Cartel. It ridicule that Cartel, calling them pigs. The page was managed by the Sinaloa Cartel.

  • August

  • Narco message: Sinaloa's Cartel

    First narco message appears in the city.

  • Stolen cars

    The number of cars stolen each year jumped by 46 percent (16,905) in comparison with 2007, when 9,041 were reported stolen. Most car makes in demand by thieves were Nissan, Ford, Dodge, and the Honda models Sentra, Maxima and Altima, Others in demand are SUVS and Caravan types with sliding doors which killers prefered for hire shootouts and street firefights. The colors prefered were white, deep blue, green and sand. None of the cars involved in criminal activity had license plates.

  • September

  • High Caliber

    The ballistics unit of the Policia Ministerial,state police, reported an increase in mortal attacks with high caliber assault rifles such as AK-47, AR-15, and high-caliber shotguns ranging from .40,.45, 9 mm, 380 and the infamous 5.57, which is known as "police killer", because it can penetrate bullet proof vests.

  • December

  • Theft rises

    Total stolen cars count rises by 46 percent from 9 thousand in 2007 to 16 thousand this year. A total of 41 police agents from the Ministerial Police, or state police, are reported killed. Thirty municipal policement were killed.

  • 2009

  • January

  • Kidnappings rise

    In 2007 there were 21 reports of kidnappings. The number tripled by 2009, when 63 kidnappings were reported to authorities. Most of the victims were high level business leaders.

  • March

  • Higher police power

    Federal police with the municipal and state forces, collaborated on the Joint Operation Chihuahua. In the end 7,500 soldiers and 2,300 federal police officers were set to defend Juarez's streets.

  • April

  • Chihuahua effort

    In the Joint Operations Chihuahua, the city of Juarez prioritized combat against the most common crimes such as kidnapping, extorsion, car theft with violence, telephone extorsion and small business robberies. Reforms and lifetime prison terms were discussed as options to end the propensity of the crimes.

  • May

  • More kidnappings

    Kidnappings statistics go up.

  • June

  • Blood Hospitals

    Because of the high number of homicides and wounded paramedics and doctors, the population asked for more security in hospitals. Four city hospitals were baptized as "Bloody hospitals".

  • July

  • Theft with violence rises

    The number of stolen cars rises by 120 percent in the first semester of 2009, as compared to the same period in the previous year. Between January to June of that year, 962 vehicles where stolen during armed assaults as compared to 420 during the previous year.

  • August

  • Panic buttons installed

    The Medical Association asks for the installation of panic buttons, closed circuit cameras and permanent police and military oversight in hospital emergency areas because of the fear of getting attacked by criminal groups.

  • December

  • 2 thousand elements reinforcement

    Two thousand members of the Federal Police are brought to reinforce security for the city and take control from the army which was awash with citizen reports of abuses. Through posters pasted around city stores, Joint Operation Chihuahua begins by offering US$133 thousand for anyone providing information that leads to the capture of José Antonio Torres Marrufo, then the leader of the Sinaloa Cartel, and José Antonio Acosta Hernández, El Diego, of the Juarez Cartel. The year closes with 2,657 victims, a 63 percent hike over numers in 2008, which listed1623 homicides. More than 60 policemen were killed.

  • 2010

  • January

  • HouseJacking: Theft with violence

    Home invasions increased. Children as well as adults are atacked in these robberies of homes with violence. Rapes also occurred during these incidents.

  • Villas de Salvacar

    15 people are murdered at Villas de Salvacar. Most of the victs were students a CBTIS 128, a local high school. The killers mistook the birthday party as one organized by members of the AAs a rival gang of the killers who belonged to the Juarez Cartel La Línea. The attack was ordered by El Diego.

  • February

  • Chaos

    Local residents were caught in the middle of the criminal confrontation of two cartels. Homicides could take place anywhere, anytime. Judges were under threat and began to ask for police assistance.

  • March

  • Consulate Employees

    U.S. Consulate officials are attacked after a children's birthday party. Three people are killed.

  • April

  • Fear in the police

    Municipal Police members fear the new killings do not respect anybody. "Before nobody touched the family. Now it does not matter who is there, they come and kill everyone, the kids and the wife," said one agent.

  • May

  • Walled Force

    Military and police units begin to operate behind their headquarters, as more of their members are targetted.

  • July

  • Ambush

    A car bomb directed to attack Federal Police goes off after the police arrest Jesús Armando Acosta alias "el 35", a blood relative of El Diego of La Linea.

  • August

  • 30 bodies per day

    30 murders were reported. In 4 years the crime rate went up a thousand percent or 10 times the numbers registered in previous years.

  • October

  • Killings Record

    Closes with a record of 352 homicides, most of them execution style, and most of the victims young people.

  • November

  • Arturo Gallegos aprehended

    Federal Police arrested Arturo Gallegos Castrellón "El Farmero", one of the top members of the gang "Los aztecas", and charged him with participating in the murder of the U.S. Consulate employees.

  • December

  • 3120 víctims

    This year, Juárez reaches the highest number of murders: 3,120 -- 500 more than in 2009.

  • 300 women killed

    Las carpetas de investigación arrojaban 300 mujeres asesinadas (incluyendo a la activista Marisela Escobedo) mientras las estadísticas señalaban que Juárez era la ciudad más violenta del país y del mundo.

Social Happennings

  • 2007

  • March

  • Authorities estimated that there were 616 gangs dedicated to crimes, mainly to drug dealing, four of them were classified as extremely dangerous, including The Aztecas, Mexicles, Asassin Artist or Double A.

  • May

  • Leaders of the gangs "Los Mexicles" and "Los Aztecas" sign a no aggression pact organized by the state human rights commission.

  • July

  • There were 400 civilian non-governmental organizations in the state of Chihuahua. At lest 300 of them were based in Juárez.

  • November

  • Gangs of the city started recruiting kids as young as 10 years to stage robberies, attacks and homicides.

  • December

  • 327,438 Juarez residents work in Juarez-based American assembly plants called maquilas.

  • 2008

  • January

  • The Army takes control of the city, implementing a security strategy code-named Joint Operation Chihuahua.

  • March

  • From 2004 to 2008 Juárez assumed Mexico's highest number of public-funded housing, reaching a total number of 110 thousand.

  • December

  • 322,737 Juarez workers were registered in the Mexican Institute of Social Security (IMSS)

  • The maquilas began massive layoffs of workers, or reduced their working week from 5 to 3 days because of the violence.

  • 2009

  • January

  • Employment at the local maquilas reached a historical low with only 166,454 workers

  • June

  • 302 thousand 365 Juarez workers were registered in the Mexican public medical system, IMSS

  • December

  • On the night of January 30, an armed commando enters the working-class neighborhood of Villas de Salvacar, killing 11 youth and four adults. Most of the victims were high school students not engaged in gangs.

  • 2010

  • January

  • On January 14 the Army's Joint Operation Chihuahua changes strategy and becomes Coordinated Operation Chihuahua, with higher presence of the Federal Police in the city.

  • Paramedics and other medical personnel working for the Mexican social security system, IMSS, march in mass through the city to protest the increasing wave of kidnappings and extorsions the medical profession was facing.

  • February

  • February 12th. María Dávila, mother of Marcos and José Luis Piña Dávila, two of the 15 victims murdered in Villas de Salvacar confronted President of Felipe Calderón: "What would you do if they were your children...", "I am not happy that you are here", "I want you to put yourself in my place...", "My two children were killed", Davila cried out.

  • 900 gangs were in place, most of them worked as sicarios for the drug cartels.

  • March

  • It is announced that the Mexican Army is in charge only of special operations in the city, while the Federal Police and Transit patrol city streets.

  • April

  • February 12th, After the massacre of Villas de Salvacar, President Felipe Calderón Hinojosa, and his wife, Margarita Zavala, visited Ciudad Juárez. The president presents the government plan "Everyone is Juárez, Lets Rebuild the City", under which authorities and civic organizations begin to work together.

  • July

  • On July 15, a car bomb explodes in downtown Juarez. Two people die, including a local doctor who tried help a victim and a police officer.

  • Juárez citizens grow upset with Federal Police presence, as they blame them for the increase in extortions.

  • October

  • The night of october 23 another masacre injuries the city in the neighborhood of the Horizontes del Sur, where 15 people are murdered in the 15th birthday of a young man.

  • December

  • 312 thousand 920 workers from Juárez were registered to the IMSS.

  • Ciudad Juárez was the mexican city with the highest number of inhabited hauses, almost 23% percent. People fleed with fear to violence and unemployment from the assembly lines.

  • The work stoppages made by the medics that work in both private and public institutions continue, this time with the support of more sectors and the catholic church.

  • While the violence was in its peak in the city, civic organizations like "Ciudadanos Comprometidos por la Paz" (Ccompaz) worked with hundreds of kids. Ccompa "recruited" 150 kids and adolescents of poor homes and armed them with a musical instrument to form a musical orchestra.

  • 2011

  • February

  • February first of 2011 the Sports Unit Villas de Salvacar was funded, a year later of the killing of 15 young kids.

  • March

  • On March 10, 2011 Ret. Army Col. Julían Leyzaola Pérez was named Secretary of Public Security(SSPM), in charge of the city's municipal police. He arrived in his new post with a dark cloud and accusations of human rights violations in his last post as police chief of the border city of Tijuana.

  • April

  • With no other major educative oportunities, mainly in high school level, cultural or of healthy recreation, in the northwest of Juárez the number of gangs and minors that commited crimes rised.

  • December

  • 313 thousand 994 Juárez workers were registered in the Mexican state medical insurance, IMSS.

  • 2012

  • September

  • Murders went down in Juarez, but citizen reports about police abuse increased,especially against youth.

  • October

  • In Juárez, there were 227, thousand children form young infants to 5-years-old. Only 3 of every 10 child had acess to the preschool services, and an even lower number to childcare, according to figures from The Autonomous University of Juarez, UACJ.

  • Municipal Police Chief Julían Leyzaola Pérez issues a controversial order to stop any person who appears suspicious and has no official ID. With this mandate policemen stopped thousands of construction workers, car-washers, "parqueros" and low-income people, who were hauled to jail for 12 hours and had to pay US$50 dollars to get out.

  • As part of the program "We all Eat", the city of Juárez distributed 400 thousand chickens in low income neighborhoods.

  • December

  • Child depression cases increased by 50 percent according to a crisis center ran by Casa Ammiga. It expressed itself in food disorders, lack of sleep and low school performance.

  • 2013

  • January

  • 333 thousand 879 Juárez workers were registered in the IMSS.

  • It is estimated that more than 10 thousand children were orphaned because of the violence of Juárez.

  • October

  • From 2010 to 2012 the Federal Government invested an approximate US$300 million dollars to invest in social programs and urban upgrade projects in Juarez.

  • Hector "Teto" Murguía Lardizabal gave his last government report at a event that cost an estimated half a million dollars.

  • November

  • During the administration of Murguia, about US$7 million dollars were given by the federal government for the construction of 50 community social centers that were to benefit more than 350 thousand people, according to authorities.

  • It was discovered that gang members were students from junior high school to college. Gang affiliation was promoted by weak family structures, according to Casa Amiga.

  • At least US$9 million dollars were not properly accounted for at the end of the Murguía Lardizabal Administrationn as mayor of Juarez, according to Chihuahua's 2012 Public Accountability Report and another technical report sent by the the State Auditor to the Mexican Congress.

  • December

  • The NGO Citizens Committed to Find Peace, Ccompaz, recruited 700 children and youth from 9 to 16 years old to participate with their musical orchestra. The objective is to keep them away from streets and violence.

  • 345,732 Juárez workers were registered in the IMSS.

  • 2014
