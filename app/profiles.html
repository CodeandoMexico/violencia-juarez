Vicente Carrillo

'Viceroy'

Born on October 16, 1962 he inherited control of the Juarez Cartel in 1997, when his brother Amado died. Juarez was among the most important organized crime groups in Mexico. Its armed group "La Línea", ruled Ciudad Juárez, an important city in the Mexican state of Chihuahua. He was arrested on October 9, 2014 in Torreon, Coahuila.


José Antonio Acosta Hernández

'El Diego', 'Blablazo', 'Diez', 'Dientón'

Former director of the Policia Ministerial in Juarez, Chihuahua until 2007, when he shifted sides and joined the criminal organization "La Linea" after the execution of José Luis Ledesma "el J.L.". In his beginnings in service of the criminal groups he worked as a recruiter. As a drug lord, he ordered several masacres in Juárez. One was the murder of 11 students and 4 adults in the working class neighborhood of Villas de Salvacar on January 31, 2010. He also ordered the building and detonation of a bombcaHe was arrested on July, 29, 2011 in Chihuahua city, the capity of Chihuahua state.


Saulo Reyes

Former Director of the Municipal Agency of Public Security between 2004-2007. A respected businessman, he was arrested in El Paso during an Immigration Customs Enforcement sting operation, after he tried to lure an agent to help him smuggle marijuana across the U.S.-Mexico entry points.


José Luis Ledesma

'El J.L.'

Worked under the command of el Diego and fell in a shooting in Sinaloa and buried in Hermosillo, Sonora. For some time he was in charge of "La Linea", making it possible to distribute sustantive ammounts of drugs.


José Alfredo Soto Arias

'El Arnold'

A former worker of a junk-yard he was one of the youth recruited as a killer by La Linea's "El Diego". He participated in the killing of Villas de Salvacar. He also participated in the murder, execution style of six persons in a Juarez bar, the Amadeus Bar. When arrests he was sentenced to more than 250 years in jail.


César Duarte

Governor of the state of Chihuahua during the period 2010-2016, when drug trafficking related violence reached its peak record of 3117 homicides. He brought new police leadership and stabilized security in the city of Juarez.


Operación Chihuahua Coordinada

In the beginning, it was called the "Operación Conjunta Chihuahua" since thousands of soldiers covered the streets, after the abuse commited to the citizen it is decided in April 8th of 2010 to turn it over and name it the "Operación Coordinada Chihuahua", which would be under the sight of the federal police, who patrolled the city and were in charge of the Center of Inmediate Response by monitoring the cameras installed by the federal government.


Patricia González Rodríguez

State Attorney General during the government of Mayor José Reyes Baeza, she was suspected of having links with organized crime. Her brother was tortured and killed, presumably as a vengence against her.


Pedro Martín Núñez

Pedro Martin, a pastor nicknamed "El Gato", works with young gang members. His mother was sent to prison when he was 2 years old. He grew up around drugs and prostitutes, however he found God and has devoted his life to rescuing young men from a life of crime.


Irma Guadalupe Casas Serrano

A psychologist, she is director of "Casa Amiga" an NGO that works with women who suffer family and genre violence.


Héctor Murguía Lardizábal

He was Mayor of the city of Juarez twice, 2004-2007 and 2010-2013, also federal congressman, actually aspiring for governor.


Barrio Azteca

A Mexican-American gang originally from El Paso, Texas, which was born in U.S. prisons and later expanded to Mexico. It is considered a criminal transnational organization.


Quitapuercos

"Eliminate Swine", an Internet page apparently managed by the Sinaloa Cartel. Announced all armed attacks against La Linea and the Juarez Cartel. The page listed names of victims before they were killed and where bodies were to be found. The word swine was the derogatory term used by Sinaloa for the Juarez Cartel.


Alejandra de la Vega

A member of a prominent Juarez family, her family operates more than 200 convenience stores that were severely hit by robberies and armed attacks during the violent period in Juarez. She is a founding member of the Security Round Table that helped pacify Juarez.


Valle de Juárez

An extension of land that runs parallel to the Rio Bravo, and a leading cotton producing area until it became a key drug trafficking launching area. Most of its towns are ghost towns because of the drug war.
