Vicente Carrillo

'El Viceroy'

Born October 16, 1962, he became head of the Juarez Cartel in 1997 after the death of his brother Amado Carrillo Fuentes, known as the Lord of the Skies. His leadership over the cartel ended in October 2014, when he was arrested by Mexican authorities. Under his leadership, the Cartel´s armed unit known as La Linea terrorized the Juarez territory. Ciudad Juarez is an important drug trafficking point because of its connection to El Paso, a city that has multiple highways connecting the U.S.-Mexico border with various U.S. states.


José Antonio Acosta Hernández

'El Diego', 'Blablazo', 'Diez', 'Dientón'

Former top officer of the extinct Chihuahua State Attorney General for Justice, also known as the state police. He left his government post in 2007 and joined La Linea, the Juarez Cartel´s armed unit. He took over after the murder of Jose Luis Ledesma, the J.L.


Saulo Reyes

Former director of the Secretary of Public Municipal Security in the Administration of Mayor Hector Murguia Lardizabal. A well-known businessman who owned radio and police equipment companies, he was detained in El Paso, Texas for drug trafficking in January 2008. He was held in a low-security federal prison in Lexington, Kentucky until January 2015.


José Luis Ledesma

'El J.L.'

He was the former head of La Linea before El Diego took the helm. He apparently died under strange circumstances in the state of Sinaloa. He was allegedly buried in Hermosillo, Sonora.


José Alfredo Soto Arias

'El Arnold'

A junk yard operator. He was born in Juarez on November 11, 1980. He only completed high school and was one of the youth recruited by El Diego who rose to become a top killer. He participated in the murder of 15 high school and college students in the working-class neighborhood of Villas de Salvarcar on January 2010. He also planned and coordinated the killing of six people in the Amadeus Bar in downtown Juarez. He was sentenced to 240 years in prison for the murders in Salvarcar.


César Duarte

Born on April 14, 1963. Governor of Chihuahua State (2010 and 2016). He took office as the violence in the city reached its highest murder statistic, 3,117 murder for year 2010. He worked towards reducing insecurity in the state.


Operación Chihuahua Coordinada

This was an integrated public security mission that was launched after the Joint Chihuahua Operation which brought thousands of soldiers to Juarez streets. On April 8, 2010, the Coordinated Chihuahua Operation brought Federal Police to Juarez.


Patricia González Rodríguez

She was Attorney General for the State of Chihuahua during the government of José Reyes Baeza. She implemented a new penal system and introduced oral trials. She was suspected of having links to organized crime. Her brother was kidnapped, tortured and murdered, because of these alleged connections.


Pedro Martín Núñez

An evangelical pastor known as El Gato. He is 38 years old and for the last few years has worked with gang youth from por families. He spent 21 years in jail, first enrering a prison a a 2 year old because his mother killed his father. He found God in prison and has been working with gangs for 15 years


Irma Guadalupe Casas Serrano

Director of Casa Amiga, she is a psychologist who worked with the families of the Salvarcar massacre.


Héctor Murguía Lardizábal

A politician who has been senador, mayor of Juarez two times, he hired Ret. Lieut. Julian Leyzaola as head of the Municipal Police. His first term as mayor was from 2004-2007 and 2010-2013.


Barrio Azteca

This gang is a Mexican-U.S. group that started in prisons in El Paso and became a transnational gang. They were involved in most major murders in Juarez.


Quitapuercos

This was an Internet page created by the Sinaloa Cartel, that sought to identify those organized crime figures linked to the Juarez Cartel. They identified victims who will be killed by Sinaloa and loaded pictures of the bodies of the victims in the page. Some of the victims had heads of pigs added to the image.


Alejandra de la Vega

Her family owns 200 small convenience stores known as Del Rio that were hit hard by organized crime. She is a top member of Ciudad Juarez Public Security Round Table.


Valle de Juárez

An extension of land that borders the Rio Grande and where major transhipment drug connections are now located. It was protected by local drug gangs connected to the Sinaloa Cartel until connections were shifted recently.
